Honey Garlic Shrimp

This take on a popular Chinese takeout menu item is much healthier than the restaurant version. It also pairs very well with Riesling â€”either sweet or dry. Serve with whole grain rice noodles. Recipe adapted from Peas and Crayons.

Ingredients

Shrimp

  • 12 oz shrimp (I use red Argentinian frozen shrimp from Costco, thawed)
  • 3 teaspoons dark sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 4 tablespoons chopped green onions
  • rice noodles for serving

Honey Garlic Sauce

  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 1/4 cup coconut aminos
  • 2 tablespoons chili sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch

Preparation

  1. Whisk together sauce ingredients in a small bowl
  2. Heat a large skillet to medium-high, add sesame oil, white parts of green onion, and garlic
  3. Cook shrimp 1-2 minutes per side until they are curled but not cooked through yet
  4. Add sauce, cook an additional minute
  5. make a cornstarch slurry by adding 1 teaspoon of water to the cornstarch. Add to shrimp mixture and stir until it reaches desired thickness
  6. Serve with cooked rice noodles
